One of the systems I first thought of was the MTA train systems. I think a lot of factors goes into how the system behaves.

  • The time of day
  • Price of a fare
  • Customer accidents/need for medical emergency
  • Need for police
  • Day of the Week
  • Unexpected stops
  • Strength of signal
  • People being competent at their job
  • Trains working condition
  • Trains staying on schedule
  • Amount of trains
  • Condition of the train stations
  • Weather Conditions

I think that for a majority of the factors, they can be controlled internally. But stuff that happens to people while they are on the trains/waiting for the Trains can absolutely affect the system as a whole. I've had to wait countless times in between stops because of medical emergencies. But I think that the things such as Ithe amount of trains, the fare, and the schedule of the trains are mostly handled internally.
Most of the things I can think of right now are short term effects, that don't usually carry over to the next day. The faire does absolutely though, and that can have lasting long termeffects on the amount of people taking the trains. The condition of the train stations and weather conditions can be medium term though.
It is easy to see how factors effect other factors in this example because of things such as police or medical emergencies have to slow down the trains to get to people. Faire effects how many people can afford to take the trains. Weather effects if the trains are even running or not.
I don't know about feedback loops but I guess 'chaos mode' would be like rush hour. A large amount of people taking the trains all at once, with limited amounts of trains. The same could be said about holidays or events like parades with heavy traffic and a lot of people attending.
